With school board members from across the state in town Tuesday, top lawmakers and schools chief Tom Luna declared his controversial teacher merit pay plan dead, citing a lack of buy in. Details of a new plan were sketched out during several private meetings throughout the afternoon.

Committees are having fun with abortion and gun rights. And toll roads. And [midwives](http://www.boiseweekly.com/gyrobase/Content?oid=oid%3A311106).

And a Michigan policy on immigrant drivers licenses, similar to one being considered by the Idaho Legislature, is shutting out thousands legitimate workers. Rick Pluta on [Michigan Public Radio](http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=18937753)&#8230; in today&#8217;s Newsrack.

* * *

**New teacher contract idea gone.** &#8220;iSTARS II&#8221; now in the works. No Category 4 contracts proposed, but ISAT is still a measure for teacher merit pay. Allen in the [Statesman](http://www.idahostatesman.com/newsupdates/story/293938.html). Merit pay for hard-to-fill positions, additional teaching duties. Miller, AP in the [Press Tribune](http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/I/ID_XGR_TEACHER_PAY_IDOL-?SITE=IDNCP&SECTION=HOME&TEMPLATE=DEFAULT).

**Rival abortion bill would felonize anti-abortion coercion.** Adds a clause to Nonnini abortion coercion bill to make coercing a woman NOT to have an abortion a crime as well. Both measures awaiting legal opinion. Druzin in the [Statesman](http://www.idahostatesman.com/idahopolitics/story/293940.html).

**Interfaith group lobbies for grocery tax credit.** Poorest couples who do not file tax returns don&#8217;t qualify for the $20 credit. Fix it. Miller, AP in the [Press Tribune](http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/I/ID_XGR_GROCERY_TAX_IDOL-?SITE=IDNCP&SECTION=HOME&TEMPLATE=DEFAULT).

**Attorney General says Moscow City violates gay marriage ban.** Recent policy extends health benefits to domestic partners. AG apparently says it&#8217;s unconstitutional, based on Idaho&#8217;s marriage definition. ACLU, city defend policy. Druzin in the [Statesman](http://www.idahostatesman.com/idahopolitics/story/293945.html). No one has applied for the benefits. State law precludes city from offering bennies. David Johnson in the [LMT](http://www.lmtribune.com/story/northwest/14809/) [subscrip].

**Just be born.** Docs oppose voluntary midwife licensing bill&#8230; [Unda&#8217; the Rotunda](http://www.boiseweekly.com/gyrobase/Content?oid=oid%3A311106) asks why.

**College kids with guns at Annex.** Debate today on superseding local gun bans. Rep. Roberts: It&#8217;s a freedom issue. Ferguson in the [LMT](http://www.lmtribune.com/story/northwest/14808/) [subscrip]. **REMINDER**: [Capitol Mall area is a no-carry zone.](http://gov.idaho.gov/mediacenter/execorders/eo04/eo_2004-07.htm) Except for Legislators&#8230;

**Lobbyists with connections.** All of them really. Lobbying in Idaho is a family affair. AP in the [IBR](http://www.idahobusiness.net/archive.htm/2008/02/13/Companies-lean-on-Idaho-insiders-to-make-government-contacts).

**What I&#8217;ve got is a brain tumor.** Sen. Clint Stennett&#8217;s tumor removed, now on to chemo, experimental drugs. Stennett: &#8220;And I intend to be working and taking the treatments at the same time.&#8221; He&#8217;s coming out swinging. Stahl in the <a href=""And I intend to be working and taking the treatments at the same time."">http://www.mtexpress.com/index2.php?ID=2005119329</a>.

**County toll roads proposal rejected.** Nice idea, but nah. State can authorize toll roads under old, old law. Russell in the [Spokesman](http://www.spokesmanreview.com/idaho/story.asp?ID=231626).

**School districts seek help for waning rural school funding.** Craig-Wyden timber money could cost rural districts thousands. Appeals to Legislature for help. Ferguson in the [LMT](http://www.paleomedia.org/2008/02/13/teacher-contracts-guns-abortion/) [subscrip].

**Vote by mail stalled, again.** Counties want permanent absentee status. Tom Loertscher, a Bone Republican, has not scheduled a hearing. Dan Chadwick, Idaho Counties Association: &#8220;We keep hearing, &#8216;Maybe this week, maybe next week. I just want a hearing on the bill.&#8221; Davidson in the [Post Register](http://www.postregister.com/story.php?accnum=1003-02132008&today=2008-02-13%2000:00:00) [subscrip].

**Senate will do naturopathic rules, then.** Alternative docs can&#8217;t get their act together. Sen. Chuck Coiner: &#8220;I&#8217;ve never seen a group as dysfunctional as this one. They don&#8217;t understand the process. They don&#8217;t understand what they&#8217;re talking about.&#8221; Poppino in the [Times-News](http://www.magicvalley.com/articles/2008/02/13/news/local_state/130691.txt).

**ICL: No public involvement in bighorn plan.** Conservation League letter: Otter plan to separate bighorn sheep and domestics is top down policy, inviting lawsuits. Depts. of Ag and Fish and Game plan due to governor Thursday. Christensen in the [http://www.magicvalley.com/articles/2008/02/13/news/local_state/130677.txt](Conservation League letter:). 

**US Fish and Wildlife wants out of sage grouse agreement.** Service says agreement could delay listing decision, but environmental lawyers want them to wait for a sage grouse report in November. Attorney Laird Lucas: &#8220;If we don&#8217;t wait for that, it means they can put science aside. We&#8217;ve already seen them do it.&#8221; Ridler, AP in the [Press Tribune](http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/W/WST_SAGE_GROUSE_IDOL-?SITE=IDNCP&SECTION=HOME&TEMPLATE=DEFAULT)

**N. Idaho silver mining on the upswing.** Sterling Mining in the Silver Valley hiring new workers, pulling out lots of silver. Garmire in the [CDA Press](http://www.cdapress.com/articles/2008/02/13/news/news02.txt).

**White men still running Boise City, Ada County.** Meridian has a few women. Boise city councilwoman Maryanne Jordan: &#8220;We have a great team, I just wished it photographed a little differently.&#8221; Kreller in the [Statesman](http://www.idahostatesman.com/273/story/293932.html).
